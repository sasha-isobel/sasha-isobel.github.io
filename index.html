Visit Iceland!

Travel Webpage by Sasha Vila Rumszewicz

Iceland is a unique nordic island, popular for its breathtaking natural beauty. It's often referred to as the "Land of Fire and Ice" thanks to the glaciers and volcanoes that can be found throughout the country.
Because of it's very northern location, the sun shines over Iceland for most of the day in the summer, but the island is in darkness for more than half of the day during winter. This makes it possible to see the stunning northern lights in the fall, winter and spring.

Best things to see/do

Visit the Blue Lagoon
The Blue Lagoon is a serene spa where visitors can relax. The water is rich in minerals like silica and algae that are very beneficial to the skin. Guests can explore the different parts of the spa, like sauna rooms, pools, skincare masks, and more!


Hike in the mountains
Hiking in Iceland is a great way to explore the natural beauty and see unique things like volcanoes, glaciers, untamed rivers and mountains.

See/swim in geothermal hot springs
Many hikes pass by small remote natural hot springs that you can actually swim in for free! Even though the air is cold, the rivers naturally steam and have geothermally heated water.


Hang out the the capital city, Rejkjavik
The capital city of Iceland is very small compared to what we're used to in North America, yet is still full of life and adventure. There are tons of delicious food options, gift shops and local businesses on the main rainbow road. At the top of that road there is the the talles church in Iceland, Hallgrimskirkja that is an iconic and gorgeous centre piece. Additionally, there is the Perlan Museum that showcases all of the natural beauty and even has interactive exhibits and a unique glacier experience.


Go whale watching
There are many different whale watching tour boats, in an affordable price range, that allow guests a remarkable, close-up encounter to see whales in the cold waters off the coast. A bunch of different species can be seen, like humback whales, blue whales, minke whales, porpoises and puffins!
