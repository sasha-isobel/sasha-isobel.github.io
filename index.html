The key elements to survive outdoors:

shelter, fire, and being prepared for the weather.

SHELTER

Shelters...intro


Here are steps to building a shelter using natural materails found in a forest environment:

  1. Select a site - Choose somewhere relatively flat, free of lose rocks, close to a source of drinking water and wood for building/burning. Try to avoid being hit by strong winds and away from bugs.
  2. The bed - Don't sleep on the ground because it will make your body lose heat! Instead, elevate yourself above the ground using logs. Then add a layer of bedding made from boughs/leaves/grass.
  3. The shelter frame- Using big sticks to build with, create an angled pitch to avoid rain/snow leaking in. Next, insulate the whole surface/in between any cracks with boughs of evergreen trees/grass/moss/bark etc.

FIRE

Fire little explanation...


Here are some steps to making a controlled fire:

  1. Carefully select a site - Make sure its sheltered from the elements such as rain, wind and snow so the fire doesn't get put out. The site should also be away from anything flammable like overhanging branches, roots, etc.
  2. Start the flame - Some fire starting tools include: a lighter, matches, a flint striker, or a fire bow . Use the fire starting tool to create embers with tinder (fluffy, dead, dry plant material like birch bark, cattails, grasses, etc.)
  3. Build with kindling/maintaining - After you have a small flame burning off of the tinder, add in small pieces of kindling (small twigs, sticks that increase in thickness)
  4. Putting the fire out - Stop adding fuel to the fire and after a while it'll start to go down. To quickly extinguish the flame, drown it with water. Mix the embers with soil and dirt, making sure it's wet so the fire doesn't start again.

WEATHER

One of the only things you can't control in a survival situation is the weather! It can be unpredictable so it's vital to be prepared and ready.

Dangerous Weather

  • Thunder/lightning: stay in your shelter
  • Blizzards/windchill: don't go out during whiteout conditions! Layer your clothing.

Region Climate considerations
