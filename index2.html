SHELTER

When you go camping, you likely bring a tent to use as a shelter. But if you find yourself in an emergency survivial situation, there are many ways to build a shelter yourself using natural materials!


Here are steps to building a shelter using natural materials found in a forest environment:

  1. Select a site - Choose somewhere relatively flat, free of lose rocks, close to a source of drinking water and wood for building/burning. Try to avoid being hit by strong winds and away from bugs.
  2. The bed - Don't sleep on the ground because it will make your body lose heat! Instead, elevate yourself above the ground using logs. Then add a layer of bedding made from boughs/leaves/grass.
  3. The shelter frame - Using big sticks to build with, create an angled pitch to avoid rain/snow leaking in. Look at the pictures to see the different frame styles!
  4. Insulation - Finally, insulate the whole surface/in between any cracks with boughs of evergreen trees/grass/moss/bark etc. And if you have one, use a tarp to cover the frame so that no rain/snow can leak in.
